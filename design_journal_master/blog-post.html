3 religion on 3 operating systems (Collage)

Published Jan 18th5 min read

This collage talks about the people's relationship with technology through the lens of religion. Three operating systems, "Microsoft", "Linux" and "Apple" are compared to three schools of religions. From the perspective of this collage, when someone chooses to purchase products of one of the operating system company, the said person is engaging in some sort of religious activity.

Design Brief

The key question this collage is addressing is that how should people make choices when confronted with the complexity of technological world?

On the one hand, the technological world has become itself a system for which religions develop and flurish. Both developers and customers adapted to a unique belief system within that company. Each one of them is drastically different than the others. On the other hand is an ordiniary consumer, who is just trying to make a choice among all the chaos.

What this collage is trying to convey is that people should do the research actively rather than passively consume the ideas sold by the companies. Consumers should take on the initiative, and base their choices on sound and solid research. The collage tries to endow some of the romantic sentiment towards the person making the choice. The collage empower the ordinary people in making choices independent of the influence of large cooperations, sociol norms and conventions

Research Process

First, I try to look for artwork that could convey a sense of power about people making choices. What immediately comes to my mind is the romanticism painting the Wanderer Above the Sea of Fog.

The painting expressed the idea of an empowered individual. However, the sense of choice has not been made clear, therefore extra layer of 3 paths was further collaged into the picture, making clear that the main potagonist is making a choice.

Moving on to the 3 operating systems represented by the religions.

The central idea in the collage of linux is to show the ideology of linux developer and user who believes in the principle that softwares should be open source and free to use and contribute. Therefore, elements such as open source logo and kali linux represent the idea of free developing and hacking. And by default, the Christ in Linux will be Linus Tolvald, who created the very core of linux. Other elements such as the computer on the grass with the commandline open equally shows how developers enjoys old fashion command line developing rather than modern age GUI interface, which almost feel unnatrual

The religious background comes from a painting where the Jeusus preaches. This could be the manifestation of how linux spread the idea of open source all around the world. Those who believes in the idea of open source consider them as the essence of religious doctrine, which they hold dear to.

For microsoft, since this is the operating system that first becomes comercialized, I added many dollar signs in the collage to imply for the profits, which they hold dear to. For those bourgeoisies who buy the operating system, they were often under the influence of how it is connected to all the office apps bundles such as Word or Powerpoint. Thus, I tried to look for a home familiar setting such as the picture where the Christ have a dinner with his family. Paul Allen is depicted to be a joyful and creative partner for Bill Gates.

For reference, I have include the orginal picture of window stained glass. When I was doing research for possible materials for the microsoft collage, this stain glass stand out to me for the elaborate texture at the frame of the stain glass. This design is quite harmonous with the basic microsoft design logo style.

Finally for the apple collage, I choose the adam and eve painting since the forbidden fruit and apple has been strongly associated together. Moreover, the entire apple community is sort of like a biosphere, which can be anagolous to the garden of eden. With airpods, apple M1 chip, apple watch, and iPads, most of the apple products has a closed life cycle where many of the products are codependent on each other. It make sense to collage the apple products into fruits that are naturally grown on the trees of eden. Steve Jobs in the picture is collaged into the snake for his rebellious and innovative spirit, while Tim Cook is collaged into a tree who cares deeply for the stability of the entire biosphere and how to squeez profits from the dwellers of the eden.
